About this Project

Welcome to the SplitEase demonstration project! This web application showcases modern front-end development techniques using HTML, CSS and JavaScript. The interface is designed to be intuitive, responsive, and visually appealing while maintaining excellent performance.

One of the core features of this application is the Rent Split Calculator, which helps roommates fairly divide rent and expenses. This feature is fully functional and uses a transparent algorithm to ensure everyone pays their fair share based on their contribution to shared expenses.

This project is primarily a showcase of UI/UX design and front-end implementation. While many of the features are fully functional on the client-side, some backend functionality is simulated or limited in this demonstration version.

Rent Splitting Algorithm

Our rent splitting calculator uses a fair and transparent algorithm to ensure everyone pays their appropriate share. Here's how it works:

  1. Step 1: Calculate the total expenses contributed by all roommates (groceries, utilities, etc.)
  2. Step 2: Calculate the average expense per person by dividing total expenses by number of roommates
  3. Step 3: For each roommate, determine if they spent more or less than the average:
    • If they spent more than average, they get credit (pay less rent)
    • If they spent less than average, they owe more (pay more rent)
  4. Step 4: Calculate each person's fair rent share as: 
    Fair Rent Share = (Total Rent ÷ Number of Roommates) ± Expense Difference

Where:
- Expense Difference = Average Expense - Individual's Expense
- If expense difference is positive (spent less than average), add it to rent share
- If expense difference is negative (spent more than average), subtract it from rent share

This algorithm ensures that while everyone pays an equal portion of the rent, those who contribute more to shared expenses are compensated fairly by paying less rent, and vice versa.

Example: If three roommates share a ₹9000 rent, and have total expenses of ₹3000 (₹1500, ₹900, and ₹600 respectively):

  • Average expense per person: ₹1000
  • Roommate 1 (spent ₹1500): Rent share = ₹3000 - ₹500 = ₹2500
  • Roommate 2 (spent ₹900): Rent share = ₹3000 + ₹100 = ₹3100
  • Roommate 3 (spent ₹600): Rent share = ₹3000 + ₹400 = ₹3400

The total still equals ₹9000, but each person's contribution is adjusted based on their other expenses.
